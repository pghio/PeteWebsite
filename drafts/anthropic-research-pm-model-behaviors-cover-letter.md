# Cover Letter — Research Product Manager, Model Behaviors @ Anthropic

Dear Anthropic Hiring Team,

A few months ago I pointed eight LLMs at my own production family assistant and ran
~2,800 calls through them to see how they'd behave. I wasn't measuring leaderboard
scores — I wanted the behavior: which models hallucinated an action when the user was
just thinking out loud (one did it 45% of the time), where every model hit a wall on
ambiguous input (~67%), and the quirks that only show up in production, like the
Claude family wrapping JSON in markdown even when told not to. I published the
methodology and open-sourced the harness.

I tell you this because it's the most honest signal I can give that this role and I
are aligned: characterizing model behavior, building evals to measure it, and turning
what I find into product decisions is what I do *for fun*, on my own time. Doing it on
Claude, with the Alignment Finetuning team, on work that shapes how millions of people
experience AI, is close to the most fulfilling thing I can imagine doing.

What I'd bring:

**A real track record of behavior research and eval design.** Beyond the benchmark, I
built an LLM-as-judge system that scores generated content for quality, spam, and PII
risk and gates publication on it — eval calibration in production. I'm now building a
behavioral taxonomy of real user requests, held to a strict data bar before I'll claim
anything from it. Taxonomies, triage, evals: this is already my instinct.

**Product judgment where there's no clean answer.** I run an agent that has to decide,
constantly, when to act, when to ask, and when to refuse. Tuning those defaults — helpful
without presumptuous, safe without useless — is exactly the nuanced, no-right-answer
work this role centers on, and I find it genuinely energizing.

**Deep, daily, hands-on AI use.** I build with these models every day. My opinions
about their behavior are evidence-backed, not vibes.

I'll name the gap honestly: my conversational-AI product is something I've built and
run solo rather than a consumer product at Anthropic's scale. What I offer instead is a
rare combination — a builder who ships, a researcher who measures before claiming, and
a product leader who's made hard tradeoffs for real users. If there's a fit, this is the
role I'd most want in the world.

Best,
