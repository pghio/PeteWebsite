# Using AI to Be Present

**Status:** Essay seed  
**Captured:** 2026-07-12

## Core idea

The strongest case for AI may not be that it lets us do more. It may be that it can absorb enough tracking, remembering, planning, and low-level interpretation that we can pay closer attention to the thing directly in front of us.

A useful AI does not merely answer questions. It holds context over time. That can turn it from another screen demanding attention into a form of cognitive scaffolding: something that remembers the thread so the person does not have to keep rehearsing it.

## Opening scene

At the gym, I was trying to understand a physical pattern that had taken weeks to notice: which muscles were stabilizing, which were acting as prime movers, and where my right side was still guarding. The AI retained the accumulated context and reflected the distinction back clearly:

> The distinction is between structural stabilization and prime-mover recruitment.

It then separated the lower trap/rhomboid complex, chest, medial delt, and triceps into different functional questions. I did not have to reconstruct the entire history, keep a training journal open, or disappear into research between sets. The machine held the abstraction while I stayed in my body.

That is a small example, but it points toward a larger design principle: **AI can preserve continuity so humans can inhabit the present.**

## Tension

Most arguments about AI and attention assume the technology necessarily pulls us away from direct experience. Often it does. A chatbot can become another infinite feed, another place to ruminate, another mechanism for replacing perception with narration.

But that is not intrinsic to intelligence. It is a product-design choice.

The same system can either:

- demand continuous engagement, or
- quietly maintain context and return only what helps the user act.

The relevant distinction is not digital versus physical. It is whether the tool consumes attention or carries cognitive load on the user's behalf.

## Possible thesis

**The best personal AI should function less like a destination and more like working memory outside the body: preserving context, reducing administrative vigilance, and returning people to the lives they were trying to manage.**

## Sections to develop

### 1. Presence has an administrative tax

A large share of distraction is not entertainment. It is vigilance: remembering what cannot be forgotten, tracking unresolved threads, planning the next transition, and repeatedly reconstructing context.

### 2. Continuity is more valuable than answers

Search gives an answer. A context-aware assistant remembers why the question matters, what has already been tried, and what constraint should remain stable. That removes the need to mentally reload the problem.

### 3. The gym example

Use the shoulder-patterning exchange as a concrete example of AI holding a longitudinal model while the human attends to proprioception and movement quality.

Important nuance: the AI is not diagnosing the body or replacing a clinician. Its value is organizing observations, distinguishing hypotheses, and preserving the thread across sessions.

### 4. The failure mode: AI-assisted rumination

An assistant can also intensify self-monitoring by giving every sensation, doubt, or interpersonal signal another round of analysis. Context retention is not automatically healthy. The product must know when to stop elaborating and point the user back toward action or direct experience.

### 5. Design principles for presence-enhancing AI

- Hold context so the user does not have to.
- Compress rather than multiply open loops.
- Prefer a clear next action over endless interpretation.
- Interrupt repetitive analysis when no new evidence exists.
- Remain available without demanding engagement.
- Measure success partly by how quickly the user can leave the interface.

### 6. Honeydew connection

This is also the deeper premise behind a family assistant. Calendars, lists, meals, chores, and reminders are not valuable because families want to spend more time managing software. They are valuable because the software can carry the coordination burden and give the family more unfragmented time together.

## Closing direction

The usual ambition for AI is to make a person more productive while they are using it. A better ambition is to make the person less cognitively occupied after they put it down.

The ultimate interface may be one that remembers enough, anticipates enough, and exercises enough restraint that the user can forget about the interface entirely.
