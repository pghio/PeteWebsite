# Cover Letter — Product Manager, Developer Productivity @ Anthropic

Dear Anthropic Hiring Team,

I'll open with candor, because I'd rather you decide from the real picture: I
haven't built Bazel-scale build infrastructure or run a CI platform for a thousand-
engineer org. What I have done is live, every single day, inside the future this
role is really about — engineers and AI agents collaborating on code — and I have
strong, earned opinions about what makes that collaboration fast, trustworthy, and
worth measuring.

Where I think I'd add unusual value:

**I've shipped AI-native developer tooling, hands-on.** I build an agentic product
solo: a 27-tool orchestration pipeline, an evaluation harness, and an AI-
acceleration layer where Claude is in the loop on nearly every change I make. I've
felt firsthand where agent-assisted development breaks down — flaky builds, slow
feedback loops, weak evals — and what it takes to make an agent trustworthy enough
to hand real work to.

**I have a point of view on productivity metrics when agents are in the loop.**
DORA and SPACE were built for human-paced teams. When an agent can take a well-
specified task to a tested, merged change, the interesting question shifts from
individual throughput to how reliably the human-agent loop closes. I'd want to
define metrics that capture that, not retrofit old ones.

**I bring product rigor and technical fluency.** A decade across a fintech I ran as
CEO and ML products at Capital One taught me to take technical platforms from
infancy to scale, partner with engineers as a peer, and translate deep complexity
for everyone else in the building.

The honest gap is large-scale build/CI/accelerator-toolchain depth, and I'm not
going to pretend otherwise — I'd be learning that fast. But the thing this role is
ultimately for, the AI-native acceleration layer that makes Anthropic the most
productive place in the world to build frontier AI, is the exact problem I think
about constantly. I'd love to talk about whether the tradeoff makes sense for your
team.
